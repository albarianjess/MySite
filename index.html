About Me

My name is Jessie Albarian, I'm 23, from Boulder, Colorado, and I am currently pursuing my undergraduate degree in Computer Science at the University of Colorado Boulder. I made this site for you, so you can get to know me and see what I am up to, but I also made it for myself to keep track of my progress in life.

When people ask me what I want to do with my life, or what I want to be when I grow up, my answer boils down to one simple phrase: I want to help people. I don't care how I am doing it or where I am or who I am working for, I just want to help. The past few years I've found myself helping other students by organizing weekly study groups, assisting professors, and tutoring high schoolers in classes like math and science. Doing those kinds of things.. it makes me really really happy. Maybe I'll be a professor one day, but for now, I am an aspiring software developer. When I'm not in school or at work I am working on personal projects, sitting at coffee shops, and running around in the mountains soaking up all of that fresh Colorado air. I'm also a runner, artist, hiker, board game connoisseur, animal lover... that's me in a nutshell :)

Creative Mind

I like to describe myself as half creative half logical. I have a strong creative side, but I also think rather logically. I paint and build websites. I draw and do math. I design maps and develop apps. It's kind of like a switch that switches on and off.. but I have control over it. I want to use my creativity to create something great one day. Maybe a non profit or program for kids.. who knows where my knowledge and experience from school will take me.

Personal Projects

The best piece of advice I've ever been given is to take on personal projects that interest me and are related to my field of study, computer science. Currently, I'm working on:

  • building an iOS and Android mobile app for a newspaper
  • creating resume templates in Illustrator
  • rebuilding a website for a newspaper company from scratch
  • working on a math study guide for when I tutor
  • maintaining a Twitter and Facebook account for
    "The Power of Trust," a school project aimed to fix
    the current issue with police riots in the U.S.
    #trustwins
  • searching for an open-source project to contribute to (Ideas? Email me.)

Taking on personal projects (even if they don't get finished) is really important.. and I believe they are as important, if not more, than school and work. Projects have helped build the person I am today, especially the ones that led to a Red Bull (or 2), an all-nighter, endless Google searches, and hours of staring blankly at code.
My advice to you? Pick something, anything, that interests you. Then go for it! Figure out what makes YOU happy.
