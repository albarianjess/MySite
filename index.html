About Me

My name is Jessie Albarian, and I'm from Boulder, Colorado. Ashesz, if you didn't already know, is my gaming name. I made this site for you to get to know me and for myself to keep track of goals, my progress, and aspirations. You can call me an artist, adventurer, athlete, creative mind, or board game connoisseur. When I'm not being a student at the University of Colorado Boulder studying computer science or tutoring at local high schools, I'm working on personal projects and running around in the mountains. I am passionate about technology and education, and one of my goals is to create an educational program to help kids with learning disabilities. Well, that's me in a nutshell :) You can find my resume here.

Creative Mind

I have a strong creative side that likes to draw, paint, take pictures, create maps, build things, and design. I have experience in Photoshop, Illustrator, AutoCAD, Unreal Engine 4, SketchUp, and Blender. I'm hoping to take a graphic design class in the near future, and I am currently very interested in web design, both front end and back end development. I just declared a minor in Technology, Arts, and Media (TAM) at CU Boulder, and I know the classes will be nothing but rewarding and informative. I will be taking an App Development class for the Fall 2015 semester. With my experience on the iOS app I'm currently building along with this class, I plan on creating more apps at the end of the year (I have too many ideas).

Personal Projects

The best piece of advice I've been given is to take on personal projects that interest me and are related to my field of study, computer science. Currently, I'm working on:

  • volunteering to build an iOS app for a newspaper
  • painting a flower piece with acrylic paint
  • working on a math study guide for when I start
    volunteering again in the fall at Boulder High School.
  • maintaining a Twitter and Facebook account for
    "The Power of Trust," a school project aimed to fix
    the current issue with police riots in the U.S.
    #trustwins

I absolutely love taking on personal projects (even if they don't get finished), and I believe they are as important, if not more, than school and work. Projects have helped build the person I am today, especially the ones that led to a Red Bull, an all-nighter, and hours of staring blankly fixing a programming bug.
